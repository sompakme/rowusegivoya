Career

Jim Breuer has multifaceted career has included stand-up comedy, television, film, and radio.

He began honing his comedic skills on the club circuit before gaining national exposure as a cast member on “Saturday Night Live” from 1995 to 1998.

During his time on SNL, Breuer became known for his energetic performances, memorable characters such as Goat Boy, and spot-on Joe Pesci impersonations.

He has continued to tour actively, entertaining audiences with his unique humor. In addition to his work on SNL, Breuer has appeared in films like the cult comedy Half Baked (1998) and lent his voice to animated features, showcasing his versatility as a performer.

In addition to his time on SNL, Jim Breuer has expanded his career by hosting shows such as “The Jim Breuer Show” and “Web Junk 20.”

Along with comedy specials and acting roles on various T.V. shows, Breuer hosted his satellite radio show for an extended period.

Known for his die-hard loyalty to the New York Mets, Breuer has gained recognition for his viral recaps of the team’s games.

Not only is he a devoted sports fan, but Breuer is also a passionate metalhead who released a music album in 2016.

His diverse interests and talents have solidified his status as a versatile entertainer in the entertainment industry.

Personal Life

For the past sixteen years, from 2005 to 2021, Breuer resided in Chester Township, New Jersey, with his wife, Dee, and their three daughters.
