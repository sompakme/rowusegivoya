Rossif Sutherland (born September 25, 1978) is a Canadian actor who has been in a number of films. His parents were Canadian actors Donald Sutherland and Francine Racette, and he is the brother of actors Angus Sutherland and Roeg Sutherland, as well as the half-brother of actor Kiefer Sutherland and his twin sister Rachel. He was born in Vancouver, British Columbia, the son of Canadian actors Donald Sutherland and Francine Racette. Aside from Timeline (in which he played Francois Dontelle), he has featured in a number of other films (as Alex).  During the tenth season of the television programme ER, he appeared in a number of episodes. Sutherland appeared in the episode Mr. Monk and the Other Detective of the television series Monk, in which he had a minor part. The filmmaker Frédéric Rossif inspired the choice of his given name. Sutherland has subsequently starred in the film Poor Boy’s Game, which also stars Danny Glover and Flex Alexander, as well as other projects. Sutherland also featured in the 2009 comedy/drama High Life, co-starring with Timothy Olyphant, as well as the 2010 comedy The Con Artist, directed by Risa Bramon Garcia, in which he co-starred with Russell Peters, Greg Germann, and his father Donald Sutherland. In 2012, he became a member of the cast of the television series King.
