What Ferries Take You From Connecticut to Long Island?

Connecticut and Long Island are neighboring regions, separated by a stretch of water known as the Long Island Sound. Traveling between these two locations can be easily done by utilizing the ferry services available. Several ferry companies provide routes connecting Connecticut to various destinations in Long Island, offering convenience and picturesque views along the way. Here are some ferries that you can consider:

Cross Sound Ferry:
Cross Sound Ferry operates a regular service between New London, Connecticut, and Orient Point, Long Island. This ferry ride boasts stunning views of the Sound and takes approximately 1 hour and 20 minutes to complete. The company offers both passenger and vehicle transport, making it a suitable option for individuals and families looking to travel with their vehicles.

Bridgeport-Port Jefferson Ferry:
The Bridgeport-Port Jefferson Ferry is another popular choice for traveling from Connecticut to Long Island. This ferry service connects Bridgeport, Connecticut, to Port Jefferson, New York. Passengers can enjoy a comfortable journey aboard the ferry while taking in the beautiful sights of the Sound. The duration of the crossing is approximately 1 hour and 15 minutes, and vehicles can also be transported.
