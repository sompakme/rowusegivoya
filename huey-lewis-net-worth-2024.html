Introduction

Huey Lewis, born Hugh Anthony Cregg III, is a renowned American singer, songwriter, and actor. He is best known as the lead vocalist and harmonica player for the band Huey Lewis and the News. The band was a significant part of the American rock scene in the 1980s and 1990s, with a string of hits that have since become classics. Early Life and Career Beginnings

Huey Lewis was born in New York City but grew up in Marin County, California. He developed an interest in music at a young age and played in various bands during his high school years. After attending Cornell University for a short period, Lewis dropped out to pursue a career in music.

Rise to Fame with Huey Lewis and the News

In 1979, Lewis formed Huey Lewis and the News. The band’s third album, “Sports,” catapulted them to fame with four Top 10 hits. Their success continued with the release of their fourth album, “Fore!,” which produced five Top 10 hits.

Acting Career

In addition to his music career, Lewis has also dabbled in acting. He has appeared in several films and television shows, including “Back to the Future” and “The King of Queens.”

Net Worth Growth Over the Years

Over the years, Lewis’s net worth has steadily increased thanks to his successful music and acting careers. His net worth was estimated to be around $10 million in the early 2000s, and it has more than doubled since then.

Other Sources of Income

Charitable Work

Impact of Health Issues on Net Worth
