What is ruching technique?

 Posted on August 10, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  611 words  |  Martina Birk
Ruching is a French term which means "to gather, ruffle, or pleat." It is a sewing technique in which fabric or ribbon is gathered in a repeating pattern to form ruffles, scallops, or petals. Ruching has traditionally been used to decorate clothing, accessories, and quilts.

What is the difference between ruching and shirring?

Ruching is made by creating two or more parallel lines of gathering. Shirring is a gathering technique done with elastic thread, which creates a stretchy garment. Elastic thread is used in the bobbin, causing the fabric to gather up when stitched.
