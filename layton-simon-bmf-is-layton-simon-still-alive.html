So, who’s Layton Simon? Well, in the world of BMF, Layton has quite the story. He spilled the beans about his life, kids, and his brush with Detroit’s professional sports scene in a chat with the Original Gangsters podcast. Back in the ’70s, on his way to a game, Layton crossed paths with a man named Dirty Diamond, sporting a blingy 20-carat ring. The ring led Layton to Harold Stinson, running a massive drug-dealing ring. Intrigued by the allure of the illegal business, Layton dipped his toes into the world of dealing at just 14 years old. Talk about a young start! By the age of 15, Layton was cruising in a brand-new 1973 Cadillac, all thanks to his knack for the streets. But here’s where the plot thickens – Layton’s life took a turn that even BMF enthusiasts find a bit mysterious.

The Layton Simon Alive Mystery: Is He or Isn’t He? 

Fast forward to BMF, and many are drawing parallels between Layton and the character Lamar on the show. Why? Well, both faced a psychiatric rollercoaster, with Layton finding himself in a psychiatric ward in the ’80s. Lamar’s character on the series echoes Layton’s real-life struggles. But here’s the kicker – as of now, Layton Simon’s true status is still under wraps. Just like many other real-life BMF characters, Layton remains a bit of an enigma. Mystery, intrigue, and suspense – that’s the name of the game!

BMF on the Small Screen: Layton Simon in the Mix 

Now, let’s talk TV. Curtis “50 Cent” Johnson’s crime series BMF is a rollercoaster based on the real-deal Flenory brothers – Demetrius “Big Meech” Flenory and Terry “Southwest T” Flenory. The duo kicked off their drug business in Detroit as teenagers, aiming for the stars in the world of drug smuggling. Enter Layton Simon, or rather, the character Lamar, portrayed by actor Eric Kofi-Abrefa.

Kofi-Abrefa got the inside scoop from Big Meech himself about the character, adding a touch of authenticity to Lamar’s on-screen presence. Lamar, a rival to the Flenory brothers, brings some serious drama to the series, giving us a glimpse of the challenges faced by those in the drug game.

Layton Simon’s Familial Ties: Siblings and BMF Royalty 

Hold on tight – it’s not just Layton in the BMF mix. Lamar Silas, based on OG Layton ‘The Beast’ Simon, is a constant thorn in the side of the Flenory brothers. In the series, Lamar’s fate remains unknown, but Kofi-Abrefa is eager to see how the story unfolds. If you’re wondering about Layton’s siblings, here’s the scoop.

Terry Flenory, one of the brothers in the Black Mafia Family (BMF), co-founded the notorious organization with Big Meech. This money-laundering entity made waves in illegal businesses from 1990 to 200Terry, the boss of BMF, had his fair share of relationships, and while he kept his kids under wraps, their identities stayed anonymous. Then there’s Nicole Flenory, the sister in the mix. Post-BMF fame, Nicole resides in Michigan, and you can bet having brothers like Terry and Big Meech made for an interesting childhood.

The BMF Chronicles: Rise, Fall, and a Dash of Drama 

Now, let’s rewind to the ’80s when Demetrius “Big Meech” Flenory and Terry “Southwest T” Flenory founded the Black Mafia Family in Southwest Detroit. By 2000, their cocaine distribution sales spanned the U.S., linked directly to Mexican drug cartels. BMF had two key hubs – one in Atlanta, run by Demetrius, and one in Los Angeles, managed by Terry. The Flenory brothers’ empire, however, faced its downfall, leading to imprisonments and legal battles. While Terry saw a sympathetic release in 2020 due to health concerns and the pandemic, Demetrius faced a federal judge’s denial for release, citing his continued promotion of himself as a drug kingpin.
